Adolf Rafalovich

One of the tenants of flat 5 in house number 77 on Bolshoi Prospekt was the teacher Adolf Rafalovich. Like the Pevzner family and Yakov Fraiman, Rafalovich was born in Mogilev.

In December 1958, he was arrested and charged under the infamous Article 58 (“anti-Soviet activity”) for “the storage and distribution of books published abroad, translations from foreign newspapers and magazines, and contact with foreigners (particularly Israeli diplomats).”

In 1959, he was sentenced to ten years in a prison camp.

The source of this information is Rafalovich's profile in the Open List archive of political repressions .

Rafalovich's trial is also mentioned by famous dissident and zionist activist Revolt Pimenov.

When he eventually returned to Bolshoi 77, Rimma, the now-grown daughter of his neighbors, opened the door — and did not recognize him.

Some years afterwards Adolf Rafalovich immigrated to Israel, becoming part of the so-called Aliyah of the Seventies. His life stands as a personal example of the zionist dissident movement in the post-war USSR.
